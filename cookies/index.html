Cookie Usage Policy

Effective Date: January 4, 2024 | Last Updated: December 24, 2025

1. Introduction

This Cookie Policy explains how SMZ Education uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website. This policy is designed to help you understand what cookies are, how we use them, and the choices you have regarding their use.

This policy complies with the EU ePrivacy Directive 2002/58/EC (as amended by Directive 2009/136/EC - the "Cookie Law"), the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) 2016/679, and the UK Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR) 2003.

2. What are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, provide analytical information, and enhance user experience. Cookies contain information that is transferred to your device's hard drive.

Under ePrivacy Directive Article 5(3), we must obtain your informed consent before placing non-essential cookies on your device, except for cookies that are strictly necessary for the provision of services you have requested.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Essential Cookies (Strictly Necessary)

These cookies are essential for the website to function properly and cannot be disabled. They do not require consent under ePrivacy Directive Article 5(3) as they are strictly necessary for providing services you have explicitly requested.

  • smz_cookie_consent: Stores your cookie preferences and consent choices for 15 minutes. This cookie is essential for GDPR compliance and remembering your cookie consent decision.
  • donationPopupClosed: Remembers if you've closed the donation banner to enhance your browsing experience by not repeatedly showing dismissed content.

3.2 Analytics Cookies (Performance & Functionality)

These cookies collect information about how visitors use our website. All data is aggregated and anonymized. These require your consent under ePrivacy Directive Article 5(3).

  • Google Analytics (_ga, _gid, _gat): Used to distinguish users, throttle request rates, and collect information about how you use our site. Data retention: 26 months for _ga, 24 hours for _gid. Google Analytics processes this data in accordance with their privacy policy and GDPR requirements.

3.3 Advertising Cookies (Targeting)

These cookies may be set by third-party advertising partners and require your explicit consent under ePrivacy Directive Article 5(3) and GDPR Article 6(1)(a).

  • Google AdSense: Used to display relevant advertisements based on your browsing behavior across websites. Google uses cookies to serve ads based on your prior visits to our website and other sites. You can opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google Ads Settings.

3.4 Functional Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization but are not essential for basic site operation. They require consent under ePrivacy Directive Article 5(3).

  • visitCount: Counts visitor sessions to help us understand site traffic patterns. This cookie does not collect personally identifiable information.

4. Third-Party Services and Data Processing

4.1 Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics to analyze website traffic and user behavior. Google Analytics uses cookies to collect data such as:

  • Pages visited and time spent on pages
  • Geographic location (country/city level)
  • Device and browser information
  • Traffic sources and referral paths

IP addresses are anonymized in compliance with GDPR Article 4(5). Data is processed by Google LLC in accordance with the EU-US Data Privacy Framework and GDPR Chapter V requirements for international data transfers.

4.2 Google AdSense

We use Google AdSense to display advertisements. AdSense may use cookies and web beacons to:

  • Show relevant ads based on your interests
  • Limit the number of times you see an ad
  • Measure ad effectiveness

You can control ad personalization through Google Ad Settings or opt out of personalized ads from Google and other providers through the Your Online Choices platform (EU) or DAA WebChoices Tool (US).

4.3 Google Search Console

We use Google Search Console to monitor our website's performance in Google search results. This service does not use cookies or collect personal data from website visitors. It only provides aggregated data about search queries, click-through rates, and site indexing status.

4.4 CPanel Visitor Logs

Our web hosting platform (CPanel) automatically collects server logs containing:

  • IP addresses
  • Access timestamps
  • Requested URLs
  • HTTP response codes
  • User agent strings

This data is processed for legitimate interests under GDPR Article 6(1)(f) to ensure website security, prevent abuse, and maintain server performance. Logs are retained for a maximum of 90 days.

5. Legal Basis for Cookie Use

Consent (GDPR Article 6(1)(a) and ePrivacy Directive Article 5(3)): For analytics, advertising, and functional cookies, we obtain your explicit consent before placing these cookies on your device. Consent is freely given, specific, informed, and can be withdrawn at any time.

Legitimate Interests (GDPR Article 6(1)(f)): Essential cookies and server logs are processed based on our legitimate interest in operating a functional, secure website, provided this does not override your fundamental rights.

6. Cookie Duration and Retention

  • Session Cookies: Deleted when you close your browser
  • Persistent Cookies:
    • smz_cookie_consent: 15 minutes
    • Google Analytics _ga: 2 years
    • Google Analytics _gid: 24 hours
    • donationPopupClosed: Until manually cleared

7. Managing and Disabling Cookies

7.1 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit our site, you'll see a cookie consent banner allowing you to:

  • Accept all cookies
  • Reject non-essential cookies
  • Customize your cookie preferences by category

Your consent is valid for 15 minutes, after which you'll be asked to reconfirm your preferences, ensuring ongoing compliance with ePrivacy regulations.

7.2 Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View what cookies are stored and delete them individually
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Block all cookies from specific websites
  • Block all cookies from all websites
  • Delete all cookies when you close your browser

Please note that blocking all cookies may prevent you from accessing certain features of our website. Instructions for managing cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

7.3 Opt-Out Tools

  • Google Analytics: Install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Google Ads: Visit Google Ads Settings
  • Industry Opt-Outs: Your Online Choices (EU) or DAA WebChoices (US)

8. Your Rights

Under GDPR and ePrivacy regulations, you have the right to:

  • Withdraw consent for non-essential cookies at any time
  • Access information about cookies placed on your device
  • Request deletion of data collected through cookies
  • Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Lodge a complaint with your data protection supervisory authority

9. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature. Currently, there is no industry standard for responding to DNT signals. We do not currently respond to DNT signals, but we provide you with granular control over cookies through our consent banner and your browser settings.

10. International Data Transfers

Cookies may result in data transfers to countries outside the European Economic Area (EEA), particularly to the United States where Google's servers are located. These transfers are protected by:

  • EU-US Data Privacy Framework certification (for Google services)
  • Standard Contractual Clauses approved under GDPR Article 46(2)(c)
  • Appropriate safeguards as required by GDPR Chapter V

11. Changes to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy to reflect changes in our practices, technologies, legal requirements, or other operational needs. When we make material changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this policy and may notify you through our website or other means. Continued use of our website after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

12. Contact Information

If you have questions about our use of cookies or this policy, please contact us at:

  • Email: contact@smzeducation.com or support@smzeducation.com

For data protection concerns, you may also contact your local supervisory authority.
